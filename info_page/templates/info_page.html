Why are we doing this?

In 2016, about 138 million Americans voted. While that sounds like a lot, it represents just 58.1% of the eligible voters in this country. In fact this number has been slowly decreasing for the past 40 years, in 1980, that percentage was over 80%. The smallest group that voted? People aged 18-24, college kids, new graduates, graduate students, etc.

Considering that it is these peoples that will be taking the brunt of many polcies that come from elections, from economic policy, student loan and education policy, climate policy, healthcare, and the job market, it has never been a better time for young people to vote. This website attempts to start that, in a contentious election year in which young people from all over the country can make a differnence. The resources here allow you to register to vote, request a mail in/absentee ballot, learn about early voting, and ensuring your vote is counted.
